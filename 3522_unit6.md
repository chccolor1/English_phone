# HIDDEN PRINTS

Privacy is not something that I'm merely entitled to, it's an absolute prerequisite.
— Marlon Brando

Many people love taking selfies and group photos. In South Korea, hand signs like the "V" pose and finger hearts are very popular. People use them at concerts, cafes, schools, and even family events. However, experts now say that these fun poses may create a serious online safety problem. New AI technology can sometimes copy fingerprints from clear photos. Because of this, people may need to think twice before posting certain pictures online.

Biometric information, such as fingerprints and face scans, is now used in many daily activities. People unlock phones, enter offices, and pay for services using their fingerprints. This technology is fast and convenient, but it can also become dangerous if criminals steal personal data. In 2026, cybersecurity experts continue to warn that digital crimes are becoming more advanced because of AI-powered tools.

Researchers explain that fingerprints can appear clearly when fingers are close to a camera. Bright lighting and high-quality smartphone cameras also make fingerprint details easier to see. If someone uploads a close-up selfie online, criminals may try to copy those fingerprint patterns. In some cases, thieves have already attempted to use online photos to unlock smart devices and security systems.

This issue is especially important in countries where hand gestures are part of photo culture. In Korea, finger hearts and flower poses are common in social media posts. K-pop idols, actors, and influencers often pose this way during public events. Fans also copy these trends when taking pictures with friends. As the saying goes, "better safe than sorry." A simple pose may look harmless, but it could accidentally expose private information.

Cybersecurity companies are now encouraging people to be more careful online. Some experts suggest avoiding close-up hand poses when posting public photos. Others recommend using privacy settings on social media accounts. Covering fingerprint scanners on devices and using stronger passwords can also help improve digital safety.

At the same time, technology companies are working to improve security systems. Some smartphones now combine fingerprints with face recognition or passcodes for extra protection. AI security programs are also becoming smarter at detecting suspicious activity. Even so, experts say users must still play an active role in protecting their own information.

The popularity of selfies and online sharing will probably continue to grow in the future. Taking photos is a fun way to express emotions and connect with others. Still, people should remember that personal data is valuable. A single picture shared online can sometimes reveal more information than expected. By staying careful and informed, users can enjoy social media safely while protecting their digital identity.
